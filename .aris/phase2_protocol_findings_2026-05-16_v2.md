## Interpretation Boundaries

- Do not claim exact reproduction of Informer Table 2. Our GELU baseline is not Table-2 matched in performance.
- Do not claim tanh is universally best. Softsign and tanh_sin win some cells.
- Separate hidden activation from output activation. Output tanh helps some ETTh2 cells but hurts Solar96 badly.
- Solar results are site/horizon placement evidence, not Informer Table 2 reproduction.
- Lee is a memory-adjusted pilot only in this version.

## Placement Summary

- ETTh1: hidden bounded activations are the credible source of improvement; output tanh is worse.
- ETTh2: output tanh can help, especially at 336, but this is dataset/horizon-specific.
- Solar96: output tanh is harmful; Solar1/96 prefers encoder-side tanh, while Solar5/96 favors hidden boundedness / encoder-side tanh.
- Decoder-only tanh is generally weak.
