Activation functions inside long-sequence forecasting transformers are usually treated as defaults, yet they can change error without changing attention, depth, embeddings, or parameter scale. We run a full activation-only Informer grid over ETTh1, ETTh2, ETTm1, ETTm2, and eight Solar sites: 36 dataset-horizon cells, nine activation configurations, and three seeds per configuration, for 972 same-protocol seed-runs. All main-table rows use batch size 32, six epochs, fixed horizon-specific Informer windows, and matched GELU baselines. Non-GELU activations are best in 34 of 36 dataset-horizon cells; only Solar3 at horizons 24 and 96 remains GELU-best. Bounded and tanh-family activations rank strongest overall: \texttt{tanh\_sin}, \texttt{tanh}, and \texttt{softsign} have the best mean ranks and jointly account for 23 best cells. The gains are largest on ETTm2 and ETTh2, while Solar gains are smaller and site-specific. The full grid also rejects a universal replacement story: output \texttt{tanh} wins several high-gain ETT/ETTm cells but is poor on average, ReLU wins only one cell, and factor-oracle diagnostics provide only weak leave-one-seed-out regime evidence. The supported claim is therefore narrow but useful: under a matched Informer protocol, activation choice is a dataset- and horizon-sensitive design variable, not a harmless default.
