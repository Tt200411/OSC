KEY: zhou2021informer
FILE: sections/1_introduction.tex
LINE: 5
CONTEXT: This paper asks whether activation-only changes can improve Informer forecasting and, more importantly, when such changes are justified. We use Informer \citep{zhou2021informer}, a transformer architecture designed for long sequence time-series forecasting, as the controlled backbone. We keep the model structure fixed and vary only the nonlinearity inside the encoder and decoder feed-forward/convolutional blocks. This separates activation effects from attention or parameter-count effects.

KEY: hendrycks2016gelu
FILE: sections/1_introduction.tex
LINE: 7
CONTEXT: Our starting hypothesis was that lightweight oscillatory perturbations, such as adding \(a\sin(x)\) to a standard activation, could help long-horizon high-volatility forecasts. The current evidence is more specific. Bounded sign-preserving activations such as \texttt{tanh} and \texttt{softsign} explain much of the improvement over GELU \citep{hendrycks2016gelu}. Explicit oscillatory perturbations around \texttt{tanh} are not globally reliable. Lee-OC variants show strong behavior in some long-horizon ETT settings but can be harmful in solar settings. These observations motivate a factor-conditioned analysis rather than a single global activation ranking.

KEY: vaswani2017attention
FILE: sections/2_related_work.tex
LINE: 4
CONTEXT: The transformer architecture \citep{vaswani2017attention} introduced attention-based sequence modeling that later became a foundation for long-horizon forecasting architectures. Informer \citep{zhou2021informer} adapts this family to long sequence time-series forecasting through efficient attention and an encoder-decoder design. Our study does not propose a new attention mechanism. Instead, it uses Informer as a fixed experimental substrate to isolate the effect of activation functions.

KEY: nair2010relu
FILE: sections/2_related_work.tex
LINE: 7
CONTEXT: ReLU \citep{nair2010relu} and GELU \citep{hendrycks2016gelu} are common defaults in deep networks and transformer blocks. Bounded nonlinearities such as \texttt{tanh} and \texttt{softsign} are older but still useful controls because they preserve sign while constraining output magnitude. Our experiments show that these bounded controls are essential: without them, a Lee or sinusoidal perturbation may appear useful against GELU while offering little or no advantage over a simpler bounded baseline.

KEY: sitzmann2020siren
FILE: sections/2_related_work.tex
LINE: 10
CONTEXT: Periodic activations and Fourier features have been studied for representing high-frequency functions \citep{sitzmann2020siren,tancik2020fourier}. Our use of oscillatory activations is different. We do not train an implicit neural representation; we perturb the feed-forward activation inside a forecasting transformer and test whether the perturbation improves forecasting error under fixed capacity. This framing makes the comparison against the unperturbed base activation critical.
